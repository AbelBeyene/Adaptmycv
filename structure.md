1. Contact & Basic Info
Full name, professional email (not a silly/old address), phone number, LinkedIn URL, portfolio/website if applicable

Location (city/country at minimum) — relevant for remote vs. on-site roles

Missing or incomplete contact info is an immediate red flag

2. Formatting & ATS Compatibility
Clean, readable font (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman) — no decorative fonts

Proper use of whitespace, consistent spacing, and bullet points

No tables, text boxes, headers/footers, or graphics — these break ATS parsers

Length: 1 page for entry-level, max 2 pages for senior profiles; flag anything over 2

File format check: PDF is visually safe, but .docx is safer for ATS

3. Professional Summary / Headline
Is there a summary? Does it read like an elevator pitch with career highlights?

Does it include role-relevant keywords upfront?

Is it generic ("hard-working team player") or specific and compelling? Flag vague buzzwords

4. Work Experience Analysis
Reverse chronological order — flag any deviations

Each role should include: company name, job title, start/end dates (month + year), location

Bullets should show achievements, not just duties — look for quantified results (%, $, numbers, scale)

Flag bullet points that only describe responsibilities without impact (e.g., "Responsible for managing…" vs. "Reduced costs by 20%…")

Jobs older than 10 years can typically be omitted or condensed

Flag unrelated jobs that don't connect to the target career path

5. Employment Gaps
Identify all gaps between roles (flag anything 3+ months unexplained)

Check for a trend of gaps vs. a single instance — patterns are a bigger red flag

Recent gaps are more significant than old ones; check if skills were maintained (certifications, freelance, volunteering)

Short stints (under 6 months at multiple jobs) = job-hopping signal worth flagging

6. Keyword Density & Relevance
Extract hard skills, tools, technologies, and certifications mentioned

Flag missing industry-standard keywords for the claimed role (e.g., a data analyst resume with no mention of SQL or Python)

Check for keyword stuffing — same phrase repeated excessively looks unnatural

Compare keyword usage across summary, experience, and skills sections for consistency

7. Skills Section
Is there a dedicated skills section? Does it distinguish hard skills vs. soft skills?

Are skills specific and verifiable (e.g., "Python, Tableau, SQL") vs. vague ("good communicator")?

Flag outdated technologies or skills irrelevant to the target role

8. Education & Certifications
Degree name, institution, graduation year — flag missing dates

Relevant certifications listed with issuing body and year

Continuing education or online courses (especially for career changers)

9. Red Flags Checklist
Run the LLM explicitly against these known recruiter red flags:

Unexplained employment gaps

Frequent job changes without context

Vague or inconsistent job titles

Exaggerated or unverifiable claims ("spearheaded global transformation")

No measurable achievements anywhere

Typos, grammar errors, inconsistent tense

Listing jobs/skills irrelevant to the target role

Photo, age, marital status included (bias risk, especially for US/EU roles)

10. Tailoring & Personalization Score
Is the resume generic or tailored to a specific role/industry?

Does the language mirror common job postings in their field?

Is there a cover letter or personal branding statement?

11. Tone & Language Quality
Active voice vs. passive voice — "Led a team of 5" > "Was responsible for leading"

Action verbs at the start of bullets: managed, built, increased, launched, reduced

Spell-check and grammar consistency (tense: past for old jobs, present for current)
